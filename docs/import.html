User Guide · Importing

Bring your reading life in

The fastest way to feel myShelfie is to import what you've already tracked. Both paths take a couple of minutes, all from your phone — export a CSV from the service, then hand it to myShelfie.

From Goodreads

Goodreads exports your whole library — statuses, ratings, dates, and shelves — as a single CSV.

  1. Open Goodreads in your phone's browser (the export lives on the website, not the app).
  2. Go to My Books → Import/Export.
  3. Tap "Export Library" and save the CSV to your device.
  4. In myShelfie, open the menu → Import from Goodreads (CSV) → Select CSV File, and pick it.

What comes in: read / currently-reading / to-read status, your star ratings, date read, and your shelves.

From Libby

Libby's Timeline is your borrowing history. Export it to backfill everything you've read through the library.

  1. Open Libby and tap your Shelf.
  2. Go to History → Actions → Export Timeline.
  3. Select CSV and save the file to your device.
  4. In myShelfie, open the menu → Import from Libby (Timeline CSV) → Select CSV File, and pick it.

One choice to make

Libby records that you borrowed a book — not whether you finished it. So on the import preview, you decide how to treat your borrows:

Sort later (the safe default) — they land on a "Libby — To Sort" shelf, so you can move each one once you decide how you actually read it.

Mark as read — if your Timeline is mostly books you finished, bring them straight in as Read.

Either way nothing's locked in — you can change any book's status afterward.

Good to know

  • No duplicates. Anything already in your library is skipped automatically — so re-importing, or importing from both Goodreads and Libby, is always safe.
  • Imports are additive. They grow your library; they never replace what's already there.
  • It's all on your phone. The CSV just needs to be somewhere your phone's file picker can reach — Files, Downloads, or wherever you saved it.
  • Not on those services? Any tool that exports a Goodreads-compatible CSV works too — and you can always just add books as you go.
