We Had It Wrong. Tracy Set Us Straight.

The format mess, and the co-founder who made us fix it.

I want to tell you about something we got wrong for a long time, and how we finally got it right. Not because it makes us look good - it doesn't, really - but because the story of how it got fixed is the whole reason myShelfie exists.

The little lie the app made you tell

From the very first version, myShelfie asked you one question about how a book came into your life: what format is it? The choices were Physical, eBook, Audiobook... and Library.

Look at that list for a second and you'll spot the problem we somehow didn't for months. "Library" isn't a format. It's not how you read a book - it's where you got it. Nobody reads a book "in library." You read a paper copy you borrowed, or an ebook you checked out on Libby, or an audiobook you streamed from your library's app.

So if you borrowed a physical book from your local branch, myShelfie made you choose: call it "Physical" and lose the fact that it was a library loan with a due date, or call it "Library" and pretend you never turned a real paper page. You couldn't say both. The app quietly forced a little lie either way.

Five words from Tracy

Our co-founder, Tracy, is also our most relentless tester - and she happens to read exactly the way myShelfie is built for: physical books, library loans on Libby, audiobooks, the whole mix. So she kept slamming into this wall. She reported it. Then she reported it again. Then a third time. And somewhere in there she said the thing that finally cracked it open for those of us building the app:

"Library is not a format."

Five words. Obvious in hindsight. We'd been so deep in how the app was built that we'd stopped seeing how people actually read.

So we tore it up

We rebuilt the whole thing around three plain questions - the ones you'd actually ask yourself about a book:

  • How did you find it? A friend, a book club, browsing the shelves. (We call this Discovery.)
  • How did you read it? Physical, ebook, audiobook.
  • Where did the copy come from? Libby, Hoopla, Audible, Kindle, a bookstore, a gift, borrowed from a friend, borrowed from the library.

And here's the part that matters most: you can answer with more than one. Borrowed it from the library and later bought your own copy? Both go in. Started it on paper and finished it as an audiobook? Both formats. Nothing makes you flatten how you actually experienced a book into a single box anymore.

Borrowing is its own thing now, separate from format - so you can borrow a physical book and still track the due date, exactly like Tracy wanted. And once you've finished a book, it stops nagging you about a return date that doesn't matter anymore.

Why I'm telling you instead of quietly shipping it

myShelfie is supposed to be the one place that records how you really read - not a tidy fiction, not a librarian's spreadsheet, the messy honest truth of it. We had a design that quietly asked you to fudge that truth, and it took our own co-founder refusing to let it go to make us fix it.

That's the job, honestly. You tell us where it's wrong, we listen, we rebuild. Tracy just happens to be the loudest, and the most right.

If you're on the latest build, open a few of your books and have a look. And if something still feels off about how myShelfie records your reading life, tell us - shake your phone, send a note. The next "library is not a format" is probably sitting in one of your libraries right now, waiting for someone stubborn enough to point at it.

Thanks for reading along.

-Andy
