Speaking Your Notes into the Margin

Sometimes typing out a long quote or reflection on a glass keyboard breaks the reading flow. Introducing Voice Dictation for personal notes.

When we launched the Libby history importer, we had a tester bring in several hundred books they had read over the years. It was a beautiful timeline, but it came with a daunting problem: going through each title to add their thoughts, quotes, and reflections by hand on a virtual keyboard was exhausting. Voice dictation would make it much easier to populate those memories, they thought—and so did we. So we set out to build a clean way to capture your thoughts hands-free: Voice Dictation.

How to Use Dictation

In the latest build of the mobile app, you'll find a new microphone icon sitting directly next to the notes field on the book detail screen. It is styled with our standard zero-radius, cream, and charcoal MCM guidelines, turning terracotta when active.

  • Open any book in your library and scroll down to the Personal Notes section.
  • Tap the Rec button.
  • On the first run, grant permissions to use the Microphone and Speech Recognition (we only request what the operating system requires to translate your voice).
  • Speak your note naturally (e.g. *"This chapter does a great job explaining the character's motivation, but the pacing feels a bit rushed."*). The button will change to a terracotta background and display On while listening.
  • Tap the button again to stop recording, or simply pause speaking. The native silence detection will automatically end the session.
  • The transcribed text will clean-append to your existing notes, and a small green Saved checkmark will appear above the text box to confirm it has successfully synced with your database.
Dictating a note is like writing in the margins of a physical book - immediate, personal, and keeping you in the flow of the text.
Silence Detection: You don't have to worry about hitting "stop" when you're done speaking. The engine monitors speech pauses and automatically terminates recording, restoring the button's default cream styling.

Telling the Complete Reading Story

Your notes are where your reading life lives. We want to make it as easy as possible to capture those margins, whether you are typing a detailed review or dictating a fleeting thought while reading on the couch.

Try dictating a few notes on your current reads and tell us how the transcription holds up.
