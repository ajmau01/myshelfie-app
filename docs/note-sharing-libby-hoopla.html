From Libby and Hoopla Directly to Your Shelf

Skip the manual search and entry. How we built share intent listening so you can add library books in two taps.

We want myShelfie to feel like a notebook you keep in your pocket, not a database you have to spend hours maintaining. Manual data entry is the enemy of reading momentum. Searching for a book title, verifying the author, selecting the cover, and choosing a shelf takes just enough taps to make you think, "I'll do this later." Later usually means never. So we decided to build a shortcut directly from the apps where library readers find their next books: Libby and Hoopla.

The Share Sheet Pipeline

If you're already browsing Libby or Hoopla on your phone, you are looking at the exact book you want to add. The system already has all the context. Instead of copying the title or switching apps to search, you can now use your phone's native share menu to push books directly to your library.

Here is how the flow works in practice:

1. Sharing from Libby

Libby makes it easy to share titles you have borrowed or placed on hold:

  • Open Libby to the details page of any book in your shelf or catalog.
  • Tap the **Share Title** button (indicated by the system sharing icon).
  • Select **myShelfie** from the app list.
  • myShelfie opens automatically, parses the shared web link, extracts the Overdrive identifier, fetches the high-quality metadata from Libby's API, and slides up a confirmation card. Tap to add.

2. Sharing from Hoopla

Similarly, Hoopla supports direct sharing for ebooks and audiobooks:

  • On the Hoopla book page, tap the **Share** button.
  • Select **myShelfie** from the system share sheet.
  • The app will automatically launch, parse the Hoopla web link, resolve the title data, fallback to Google Books API to pull cover images and publisher summaries, and prompt you to confirm the addition.
By registering myShelfie as a native sharing handler, we bridge the gap between library discovery and personal organization.
No Duplicate Guarantee: We compare incoming shared titles against what's already on your shelves. Re-sharing a book you already own will safely trigger an "Already in Library" warning, protecting your reading timeline from cluttered duplicate entries.

Focusing on Frictionless Inputs

Building the share sheet pipeline is part of our commitment to making cataloging as frictionless as possible. It is about allowing the app to run in the background of your reading life, letting you record your library imports with minimal interruption.

Give it a try on your next library borrow and keep sending us feedback.
