Getting the most out of myShelfie

A short, growing guide. myShelfie is built to capture how you actually read — across formats and sources — so the more honestly you log, the more your library tells you back.

01Install & sign in

myShelfie is in private testing. You'll get an invite link — on iOS it opens in TestFlight; on Android it comes through Firebase App Distribution (you may need the App Tester app the first time). Once installed, sign in with Apple, Google, or email + password. Your library syncs across every device you sign in on.

02Start your library

A brand-new library opens to a Start your library chooser with three ways in:

  • Import from Libby — export your Libby Timeline as a CSV and bring your borrowing history straight in.
  • Import from Goodreads — the classic CSV export, mapped into your shelves.
  • Add a book — search and add one at a time to get going immediately.
Tip: imports are the fastest way to feel the app — your Portrait starts taking shape the moment there are books to read it from.

New to CSV exports? Here's the full step-by-step for both sources → Importing your library.

03Add a book

Tap Add a Book to search by title or author, or use the barcode scanner to scan a cover's ISBN. Pick the match, and myShelfie pulls the cover and details. If a book's already in your library, it'll tell you rather than make a duplicate.

04Format & source — the heart of it

Every book carries how you read it (physical, library, audiobook, ebook) and where it came from as a pair. A single book can hold more than one — listened to the first half on Audible, finished the back half in paperback. Add a format from the book's detail screen, and set its source (including Libby or Hoopla for borrowed ebooks and audiobooks).

You can also record provenance — how the book found you: a friend's recommendation, a book club, browsing, or an in-app pick. It's part of your portrait, not just a tag.

05Library loans & due dates

A library-format copy can carry a due date. Tap the loan on the book's detail screen to set or edit it. myShelfie surfaces active loans on your Reading (Today) screen and gives a gentle nudge when one's overdue — and once you mark a book Read, it stops nagging you to return it.

06Status, ratings & DNF

Move a book through To Read → Currently Reading → Read, give it a star rating, add notes and tags. And when a book isn't working, mark it DNF (Did Not Finish) with a reason — that's first-class here. Not finishing is part of your reading story, and it makes recommendations smarter, not poorer.

Why ratings matter: your Portrait and your picks are built from the books you've rated or finished — so rating as you go is what brings it to life.

The more you give it, the more it gives back.

myShelfie begins as a record and grows into a mirror — but only as fast as you feed it. Every rating, every format and source, every did-not-finish-with-a-reason is a brushstroke.

A handful of honest ratings in, your taste profile starts to take shape. A little further, the patterns sharpen — the genres you return to, the formats you reach for, what you abandon and why. And once your library is deep enough to truly know you, it begins pointing you toward what to read next — and telling you why.

None of it is busywork. The details you add aren't data entry — they're the raw material your Portrait and your picks are made of. Log honestly, and myShelfie can show you something true.

07Your Portrait

Open Discover and Portrait to see your reading life reflected back: a taste profile that reads like a mirror (here's what your reading reveals about you), a year-by-year reading narrative, an "on this day" memory of books you finished on this date in past years, and explained picks that tell you why each one surfaced.

08Your Circle

The Circle is small and trusted by design — invite a few readers you actually swap books with. You'll see where your tastes overlap and can pass picks back and forth. No follower counts, no public reviews — just honest portraits between people who trust each other.

09Send feedback

This is what testing is for. Shake your phone anywhere in the app to open the reporter — it captures a screenshot of exactly what you're looking at (including any open sheet) so you don't have to describe it. Send a bug report when something's off, and tell us what you were trying to do. Your reports go straight to the maker.
