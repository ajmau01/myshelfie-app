Why an invented name

Every recognizable book or reading word is already a competing app — Shelfie, Pagebound, Anthology, Orbit. Ownable means invented.

01

The name's job isn't to explain

That's the tagline's job. The name's job is to be distinctive and ours. Clarity comes from name + tagline + icon together.

02

"What does it mean?" is the point

What does "Spotify" mean? Nothing — until the product poured meaning in. An invented word is an empty vessel we get to fill.

03

Recognizable = unownable

A descriptive name (e.g. "BookSeek") can't be trademarked and vanishes among a thousand look-alikes. Coined words stand apart and are defensible.

The two finalists

Both built on the same root — the sea / the vessel filled by reading currents (Irish Lir, the deep source). Two different souls.
